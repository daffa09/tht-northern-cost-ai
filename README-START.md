# Northern Coast Beverages — Senior AI Platform Engineer Take-Home

> **Read `START-HERE.md` first** if you haven't. This README is the detailed version; START-HERE has the AI-tool starter prompt to get oriented in 60 seconds.

Welcome, and thanks for getting to this stage.

This assessment has **three tests** and a follow-up **live debrief**. The tests measure different skills:

| Test | What it tests | Suggested time |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Test 1: Build** | Can you ship working AI infrastructure quickly? | 30-60 min |
| **Test 2: Research** | Can you navigate an open problem and verify what AI tells you? | 60-90 min |
| **Test 3: Critique** | Can you find what's wrong with someone else's confident-sounding work? | 60-90 min |

Total: **2.5-4 hours of work.** Track your time honestly; we discuss it in the debrief.

## Three things before you start

1. **Use AI freely** — Claude, Cursor, Codex, whatever you use day-to-day. We're hiring for the role of "directs AI to ship real systems," not "writes code from scratch." The signal is what you do with AI, not whether you used it.

2. **There is no single right answer** in any of these tests. Strong candidates and weak candidates produce visibly different work on the same brief.

3. **Show your reasoning.** In each test, we read the *thinking* more carefully than the *output*. Decision logs, calibrated uncertainty, where you overrode AI suggestions — those are the load-bearing signals.

## How to navigate

- **`test-1-build/`** — Start here. ~30-60 min. Build and deploy.
- **`test-2-research/`** — Open problem. ~60-90 min. Research and recommend.
- **`test-3-critique/`** — Critique an RFC. ~60-90 min. Read, find what's wrong, defend your call.

Each test folder contains a `BRIEF.md` with the full brief and what to submit. Test 3 also includes a `RFC-TO-CRITIQUE.md` — the document you're evaluating.

You can take the tests in any order, on any schedule. They don't depend on each other. Submit when ready — we'd suggest within a week of receiving this.

## How to submit

Per test, place your deliverables inside the test's folder:

```
test-1-build/
├── BRIEF.md (provided)
└── submission/         ← your work here
    ├── (code)
    ├── README.md
    └── DEPLOY.md (or live URL)

test-2-research/
├── BRIEF.md (provided)
└── submission/
    └── RESPONSE.md     ← your work here

test-3-critique/
├── BRIEF.md (provided)
├── RFC-TO-CRITIQUE.md (provided)
└── submission/
    └── CRITIQUE.md     ← your work here
```

Zip the whole `candidate/` folder (with your submissions inside) and email it back. Include in your email:
- Total time spent across the three tests
- Anything unusual about how you worked
- Any questions you'd want to discuss in the debrief

## What happens after you submit

We read async (~2-3 business days). Strong submissions are invited to a **60-90 minute live debrief**. The debrief is where the hire decision happens — we walk through your submissions, push back on specific decisions, and do one small live extension with AI tools open.

The take-homes get you to the door. The conversation makes the call.

## Questions

If anything is unclear, ask. Your questions (and the choices you make in their absence) are themselves signal we appreciate.

Good luck.
